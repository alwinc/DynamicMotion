  • Feral animals impact on native species by predation, competition for food and shelter, destroying habitat and may spread diseases. Typically, feral animals may also have high reproductive rates. Consequently, their populations can increase rapidly if conditions are favourable. The purpose of this website is to help NSW Parks and Wildlife track feral pest in a more efficient and effective way. Users can register a new feral pest, update an existing feral pest, record and update feral pest sightings. Feral animals causing most public concern and economic damage includes: Red Fox: Mostly sighted in mainland Australia and small numbers in Tasmania European rabbit: Throughout Australia Feral cat: Throughout Australia, except in tropical rain forests Feral rat: Sighted Throughout Australia Feral dog: Mostly sighted in urban areas of Australia
